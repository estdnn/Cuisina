TURON

Turon is a Filipino snack made of ripe saba bananas (a variety of plantain), jackfruit, and brown sugar, all wrapped in a spring roll wrapper and fried to crispy perfection. This sweet treat is often enjoyed as a merienda (afternoon snack) and is a favorite among street vendors in the Philippines.

Ingredients

  • 4 ripe saba bananas, sliced into halves or thirds
  • 1/2 cup jackfruit, sliced into strips (optional)
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 10-12 spring roll wrappers
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  • Peel and slice the saba bananas into halves or thirds, depending on their size.
  • If using, slice the jackfruit into thin strips.
  • Place a spring roll wrapper on a flat surface, and sprinkle a little brown sugar in the center.
  • Place one slice of banana and a few strips of jackfruit (if using) on top of the sugar.
  • Fold the sides of the wrapper over the filling, then roll it up tightly, sealing the edges with a bit of water.
  • Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Once hot, fry the turon, turning occasionally until the wrappers turn golden brown and crispy.
  • Once crispy, remove from the pan and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  • Serve the turon while warm. The crispy wrapper and the sweet, caramelized banana and jackfruit filling make this treat irresistible!

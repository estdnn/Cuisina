Pancit Palabok

Pancit Palabok is a traditional Filipino noodle dish made with rice noodles topped with a rich garlic sauce, shrimp, pork, and egg. It’s a comfort food often served during celebrations or casual gatherings. The savory flavors and colorful presentation make it a beloved dish among Filipinos.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb rice noodles (bihon or palabok noodles)
  • 1/2 lb pork belly, sliced into small pieces
  • 1/2 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1/2 cup pork broth or water
  • 1/4 cup fish sauce
  • 1/4 cup bagoong (fermented shrimp paste)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon annatto powder (atsuete) or 1 tablespoon annatto oil
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, sliced
  • 1/4 cup chicharrón (crispy pork skin), crushed
  • Green onions, chopped for garnish
  • Lemon or calamansi wedges for serving

Steps

  • Cook the rice noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  • In a pan, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Sauté the garlic until golden brown and fragrant.
  • Add the sliced pork belly and cook until it turns golden and crispy. Remove the pork from the pan and set aside.
  • In the same pan, add the shrimp and cook until pink. Remove the shrimp and set aside with the pork.
  • Add the annatto powder (or annatto oil) to the pan, followed by the fish sauce, bagoong, and pork broth. Stir well to combine.
  • Let the sauce simmer for 5-10 minutes, adjusting the consistency as needed with a little more broth or water.
  • In a large serving platter, arrange the cooked rice noodles and pour the garlic sauce mixture over them. Top with the cooked shrimp, pork, hard-boiled eggs, chicharrón, and green onions.
  • Serve with lemon or calamansi wedges on the side for added flavor.
